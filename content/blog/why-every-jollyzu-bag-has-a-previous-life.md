**Every raincoat starts with excitement.**

We buy it, we love it, we wear it. Then, one day, it gets pushed to the back of a wardrobe, donated, or simply forgotten.
For many clothing items, there comes a point when they are no longer wanted. That's where JollyZu begins.

## Giving Unwanted Textiles a Second Chance

My job is to find these unwanted raincoats, jackets, waterproof fabrics, and other reclaimed textiles, and give them a second life. I source materials through donations, second-hand shops, and online platforms, looking for fabrics that still have plenty of life left in them.

## Why I Started JollyZu

The idea of creating something beautiful from discarded materials has been with me for years. I finally had the opportunity to turn that passion into reality and build a business around it. My goal isn't to claim that I can save the world, but to make a small, meaningful contribution by reducing textile waste and keeping valuable materials out of landfill.

## What Happens to Unwanted Clothing?

Many people assume that donated clothing simply finds a new home. While some of it does, a significant amount is exported overseas, where it can end up in enormous textile waste sites. Some discarded fabrics eventually find their way into waterways and oceans, creating environmental problems that affect communities and ecosystems around the world.

## From Raincoat to Bag

By rescuing materials that already exist, I can create products without relying on newly manufactured fabrics.
**Every JollyZu bag carries a story**.
Before becoming a bag, it may have been a waterproof jacket worn on rainy walks, a colourful raincoat loved by a child, or an outdoor garment that had simply reached the end of its original purpose. Instead of becoming waste, it becomes something useful again.
